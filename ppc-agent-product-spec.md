# PPC AI Agent — Product Specification

**Version:** 1.0 — MVP  
**Status:** Draft  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Owner:** Product Team

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The PPC AI Agent is an AI-powered optimisation assistant that connects to Google Ads (and optionally Microsoft Ads) to surface actionable insights, automate routine optimisation tasks, and guide advertisers toward higher ROAS. The agent is powered by Claude (Anthropic) and communicates with ad platforms via their official APIs.

The MVP focuses on read-only campaign analysis and AI-generated recommendations. Post-MVP phases introduce automated bid adjustments, scheduled reporting, and multi-platform management.

---

## 2. Goals & Success Metrics

### 2.1 Primary Goals

The core purpose of the MVP is to reduce the time an advertiser spends on weekly PPC optimisation from an industry average of 5–8 hours to under 1 hour, while improving account performance through AI-surfaced insights that would otherwise require senior expertise.

### 2.2 Success Metrics (MVP, 90-day post-launch)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Weekly active users | 50+ |
| Average session length | 8+ minutes |
| Recommendations acted upon | >40% of surfaced recommendations |
| ROAS improvement (cohort) | +15% vs baseline |
| NPS | 45+ |
| API uptime | 99.5% |

## 3. User Personas

**Primary — Independent PPC Manager (Agency or In-House)**  
Hannah manages 5–20 Google Ads accounts for SME clients. She is technically proficient but time-poor. She needs rapid insights across accounts without switching between 6 browser tabs.

**Secondary — Business Owner Running Own Ads**  
David runs Google Ads for his e-commerce store. He understands the basics but lacks the depth to spot structural issues, poor quality scores, or bid inefficiencies. He benefits most from plain-English explanations alongside recommendations.

**Tertiary — Senior PPC Strategist at Agency**  
Priya oversees a team managing £500k+ monthly ad spend. She needs executive-level summary views and the ability to delegate action items generated by the AI to her team.

## 4. User Stories

### 4.1 Core (MVP)

1. As a PPC manager, I want to connect my Google Ads account via OAuth so that the agent can access my live campaign data.
2. As a user, I want to ask the AI free-form questions about my campaign performance and receive specific, numbered recommendations.
3. As a user, I want to see a summary dashboard of my account KPIs (spend, conversions, ROAS, QS) updated daily.
4. As a user, I want the AI to proactively flag urgent issues (e.g. disapproved ads, overspending campaigns, quality scores below 4).
5. As a user, I want to export AI-generated recommendations as a PDF or shareable link.

### 4.2 Phase 2

6. As a user, I want the AI to suggest specific bid adjustments with estimated impact, which I can approve with one click.
7. As a user, I want to receive a weekly automated email digest of AI recommendations.
8. As a user, I want to connect Microsoft Ads in addition to Google Ads.
9. As a user, I want to compare performance across accounts (for agencies managing multiple clients).

### 4.3 Phase 3

10. As a user, I want the AI to auto-apply low-risk optimisations (e.g. adding negative keywords from search term reports) on a schedule with my approval.
11. As a user, I want anomaly detection that alerts me if spend or conversion rate deviates unexpectedly.

## 5. Feature Specification

### 5.1 Authentication & Onboarding

The onboarding flow must complete in under 3 minutes. It consists of:

1. **Sign up** — email/password or Google SSO.
2. **Google Ads OAuth 2.0** — user authorises read (and optionally write) access to their Google Ads account(s). The app must request only the minimum required scopes.
3. **Account selection** — if the user has an MCC (Manager Account), allow selection of child accounts.
4. **Initial sync** — pull 30 days of campaign data. Show a progress indicator.
5. **First insight** — surface the single most impactful recommendation immediately upon completion.

### 5.2 Dashboard

The dashboard provides a single-screen overview:

- Total spend, conversions, ROAS, CPC, CTR, and impression share for the selected date range.
- Campaign-level performance table with colour-coded health indicators (green/amber/red) based on configurable thresholds.
- 30-day sparklines for spend and conversions.
- Alert banner listing any flagged issues.

### 5.3 AI Chat Interface

The chat interface is the primary interaction surface:

- Users can ask free-form questions in plain English (e.g. "Why is my Generic campaign losing impression share?").
- The AI has access to all synced campaign data and uses it as context.
- Responses are formatted with a brief summary, numbered recommendations, and estimated impact.
- Conversation history is preserved within a session and optionally across sessions.
- Users can attach CSV exports from Google Ads for ad hoc analysis.

### 5.4 Recommendations Engine

The agent surfaces proactive recommendations without being asked. Recommendation categories include:

- **Bid strategy** — campaigns not meeting target CPA/ROAS thresholds; manual bids where Smart Bidding would improve performance.
- **Quality score** — keywords with QS below 5, with specific landing page and ad copy improvements.
- **Negative keywords** — search terms generating clicks but zero conversions over 30+ days.
- **Budget** — campaigns hitting budget limits before end of day; under-funded campaigns with high conversion rates.
- **Ad strength** — responsive search ads with "Poor" or "Average" strength.
- **Audience** — audience segments with high conversion rates not being separately bid adjusted.

Each recommendation includes: priority (P1–P3), category, specific entity affected (campaign/ad group/keyword), recommended action, and estimated monthly impact in £ and/or conversion delta.

### 5.5 Alerts & Notifications

- In-app notification bell for new issues.
- Optional email alerts for P1 issues (e.g. disapproved ads, campaigns paused due to billing).
- Weekly summary email with top 5 recommendations.

## 6. Technical Architecture

### 6.1 System Overview

```
User Browser
    │
    ▼
Next.js Frontend (Vercel)
    │
    ├──► Claude API (Anthropic) — AI reasoning & recommendations
    │
    ├──► Backend API (Node.js / Fastify on Railway or Fly.io)
    │         │
    │         ├──► Google Ads API (OAuth 2.0)
    │         ├──► PostgreSQL (campaign data cache, user accounts)
    │         └──► Redis (session cache, rate limiting)
    │
    └──► Scheduled Jobs (daily sync, weekly digest)
```

### 6.2 Frontend

- **Framework:** Next.js 14 (App Router)
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS
- **State management:** Zustand for global state, React Query for server state
- **Auth:** NextAuth.js with Google OAuth provider
- **Charts:** Recharts
- **Deployment:** Vercel

### 6.3 Backend

- **Runtime:** Node.js 20 LTS
- **Framework:** Fastify (chosen for performance over Express)
- **ORM:** Prisma with PostgreSQL
- **Queue:** BullMQ (Redis-backed) for async jobs
- **Deployment:** Railway (MVP) → AWS ECS post-scale

### 6.4 Database Schema (Key Tables)

**users** — id, email, name, created_at, plan  
**google_ads_connections** — id, user_id, customer_id, access_token, refresh_token, token_expiry  
**campaigns** — id, connection_id, google_campaign_id, name, status, budget, synced_at  
**campaign_metrics** — id, campaign_id, date, impressions, clicks, conversions, cost, conversions_value  
**recommendations** — id, user_id, category, priority, entity_type, entity_id, description, estimated_impact, status (pending/actioned/dismissed), created_at  
**conversations** — id, user_id, messages (JSONB), created_at

### 6.5 Google Ads API Integration

The app uses the Google Ads API v17. Key report types used:

- `CampaignReport` — daily metrics per campaign
- `KeywordReport` — keyword-level performance including quality score components
- `SearchTermReport` — raw search terms triggering ads
- `AdReport` — individual ad performance and approval status
- `AssetReport` — responsive search ad asset performance
- `AudienceReport` — audience segment performance

OAuth scopes required: `https://www.googleapis.com/auth/adwords`

API calls are made server-side only. Tokens are stored encrypted (AES-256-GCM) in the database. Token refresh is handled transparently.

Rate limits: The Google Ads API enforces per-day and per-hour quotas. The app implements exponential backoff and queues non-urgent requests.

### 6.6 Claude API Integration

The Claude API is called with a structured system prompt containing:

1. The agent's role and capabilities.
2. The user's account summary (campaigns, recent metrics, active alerts).
3. Instructions on response format.

Campaign data is injected into the context window as a condensed JSON summary. For large accounts (50+ campaigns), only the most recent 30 days and the top/bottom 20% performers by ROAS are included to stay within context limits.

The proactive recommendations engine runs as a scheduled job and calls Claude with a batch analysis prompt, storing the output in the `recommendations` table.

## 7. Security & Compliance

- All data in transit encrypted via TLS 1.3.
- Google Ads tokens stored encrypted at rest (AES-256).
- Users can revoke Google Ads access at any time; this triggers immediate token deletion.
- No advertising data is used to train AI models.
- GDPR: data processing agreement available; EU data stored in eu-west-1 (AWS Ireland) or equivalent.
- SOC 2 Type II compliance targeted for Phase 2.

## 9. MVP Scope Constraints

The following are explicitly out of scope for the MVP to ensure on-time delivery:

- Automated bid changes (read-only in MVP)
- Microsoft Ads integration
- Multi-user / team access
- White-label options
- Mobile app (responsive web only)
- A/B testing of AI recommendations

## 10. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Ads API rate limits | Medium | High | Queue non-urgent calls; cache aggressively |
| Claude context window exceeded for large accounts | Medium | Medium | Paginate data; summarise before injecting |
| OAuth token expiry causing data gaps | Low | Medium | Proactive refresh + alerting |
| User acts on bad recommendation | Low | High | Clearly state "estimated" impact; recommend review before applying |
| GDPR breach | Low | Very High | Legal review pre-launch; DPA in place |
